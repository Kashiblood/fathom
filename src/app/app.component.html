↑CO2 ↑Temperature ↓Ice Caps ↑Sea Level

40%

With the world population increasing, so does our polution.

The increased CO2 emissions results in a greenhouse effect raising the mean tempature of earth.

As a result polar ice caps are melting and increasing the sea level.

Approximately 40% of people live within 100km of the coast.

cite: https://sedac.ciesin.columbia.edu/es/papers/Coastal_Zone_Pop_Method.pdf

These people are at risk of being displaced due to the receeding coastline.

Thus a method to inform them is needed to prevent waves of refugees as they are impacted.

start showing app demo

To address this we have created Fathom.

By providing a simple and friendly interface it is designed to be usable by children through to the elderly.

The sea level predictions are taken care of by the app behind the scenes to reduce the barier of entry that could be science literacy.

This way they can make informed decisions where they, or their decendents, should live before they would be impacted.

end app demo

What next?

Incorperating live sea level data, regonal predictions, and the Google Maps API, we could provide constantly updating predictions as climate conditions change to more specific areas.

We would also like to incorperate additional datasets for other environmental hazards, such as tsunami and droughts, to further increase awareness.

Additionally, moving beyond humans we could provide information on endangered animals in a similar fashon with predicted extinction dates.
